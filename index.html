02 · User Requirements

Show who we design for, how they experience GoRunners, and what they actually need.

This section connects our stakeholder analysis, personas, user journey pain points, and three must-have requirements for a beginner-friendly running experience.

Primary Persona

Emily is a university student who wants to build a regular running habit for fitness, stress relief, and personal achievement.

Age: 20 Role: XJTLU student Goal: Build a running habit
  • ✓Needs: Fast onboarding, simple run tracking, visible progress, and motivating feedback.
  • ✓Pain points: Existing running apps can feel boring, overwhelming, or too competitive for beginners.
  • ✓Motivation: Wants running to feel enjoyable, rewarding, and easy to continue every week.

Secondary Persona

Mr. Li is a retired older adult who does light daily jogging to maintain his health and wants a simple, safe, and encouraging experience.

Age: 67 Role: Retired resident Goal: Maintain a healthy routine
  • ✓Needs: A simple interface, clear jogging guidance, easy progress tracking, and health-friendly support.
  • ✓Pain points: Many running apps are too complex, too competitive, or difficult for older adults to use confidently.
  • ✓Motivation: Wants a tool that helps him keep a safe and regular jogging habit for better health.

GoRunners User Journey Map

Pain-point focused

This journey focuses on how a beginner runner discovers GoRunners, gets training support, joins a suitable run, participates in the activity, and decides whether to return.

Discover

Goal: Understand what GoRunners offers and whether it is suitable for a beginner runner.

Action: Visit the homepage, scan the navigation, and look for a clear starting point.

Pain point: the user may not know whether to start with finding a run, using the training assistant, or browsing the map.

Design response: Highlight clear entry paths such as Get Training Help and Find a Run.

Plan

Goal: Get a safe and practical weekly running plan based on current ability and personal goals.

Action: Open the training assistant, enter running level, goal, and condition, then ask for guidance.

Pain point: beginners may struggle to describe their goals clearly and may worry that the advice is too difficult or unrealistic.

Design response: Use structured inputs and supportive prompts to generate beginner-friendly plans with clear pacing, warm-up, and recovery advice.

Join

Goal: Find a suitable running activity that matches fitness level, confidence, and motivation.

Action: Explore runs, compare distance, pace, time, and route details, then decide whether to join an event.

Pain point: it can be difficult for beginners to judge which event is suitable, especially when they are unsure of their own pace or stamina.

Design response: Show clear labels for difficulty, pace range, distance, and beginner-friendly suitability.

Participate

Goal: Attend the activity smoothly and feel supported during the run.

Action: Arrive at the meetup point, follow the route or map, and complete the running activity.

Pain point: the user may feel nervous or unsupported if route guidance, meetup details, or activity flow are unclear.

Design response: Provide simple route information, visible meetup details, and lightweight participation support such as map guidance or check-in feedback.

Return

Goal: Reflect on progress, stay motivated, and continue using GoRunners.

Action: Review the run, ask follow-up questions, check progress, and decide whether to join another run.

Pain point: without encouragement, social feedback, or clear next steps, the user may lose motivation and not return.

Design response: Use post-run feedback, recovery suggestions, progress visibility, and community interaction to encourage continued engagement.

Must-have 01

Clear starting guidance

The system should help new runners quickly understand where to begin, whether that is getting a training plan, finding a suitable run, or exploring the platform.

Must-have 02

Beginner-friendly support

The system should provide realistic training suggestions, appropriate activity information, and supportive wording so beginner runners feel safe and confident.

Must-have 03

Motivation and continuity

The system should encourage users to return through progress feedback, recovery support, and clear next-step recommendations after each interaction or run.
